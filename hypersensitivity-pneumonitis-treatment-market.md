<p><p>The hypersensitivity pneumonitis treatment market is categorized into three types: acute, subacute, and chronic. Acute hypersensitivity pneumonitis occurs suddenly after exposure to an antigen and usually resolves with prompt treatment, focusing on eliminating the source of exposure and managing symptoms. Subacute cases develop gradually, requiring ongoing management and potential immunosuppressive therapy. Chronic hypersensitivity pneumonitis results from prolonged exposure and often necessitates long-term treatment strategies, including corticosteroids and pulmonary rehabilitation, to alleviate symptoms and preserve lung function.</p></p>
