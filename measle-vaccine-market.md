<p><p>The measles vaccine market primarily consists of two types: inactivated and attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens to stimulate an immune response without causing disease, offering safety in vulnerable populations. Attenuated vaccines, on the other hand, utilize weakened live viruses to generate a stronger, longer-lasting immunity. Both types are essential for measles prevention, with the attenuated approach providing robust immunity. The demand for these vaccines is driven by public health initiatives and awareness of measles outbreaks.</p></p>
